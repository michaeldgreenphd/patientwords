Where the answer forms

Most of this site measures what is lost when a health question is asked in everyday words instead of clinical terms. This page shows where it is lost. A language model builds its answer in stages: 26 layers, each refining the last. The Jacobian lens reads each layer as a ranked list of the words the model is leaning toward at that stage. The clinical answer counts as readable at a layer when it appears in that layer’s top 8. The methods page explains the instrument and its causal check.

Every measured set

Follow a pair through the layers under everyday wording and its outcome lands in one of three classes.

kept
the answer is still readable at the output layer.
lost late
the answer becomes readable in the middle layers, then drops out before the model speaks. The model did the work and lost the result.
never formed
the answer is not readable at any layer.

One row of squares per class, one square per pair. New sets join as the nightly cycle measures them.

Does translation work where it should?

Every answer sits on a ladder of care urgency, from reassurance up to urgent care. Under everyday wording the expected rung drops. For some pairs an LLM rewrites the everyday sentence in clinical terms and the model is measured again on the rewrite: the translated panel. Recovery is the rungs regained, the expected care-urgency of the translated sentence minus the everyday one (probability-weighted, owner-reviewed v1 tiers, domain review pending).

The depth classes make a testable prediction. Translation should regain more where the model computed the answer and dropped it than where the answer never formed: in the first kind there is finished work to recover. The table tracks that prediction as translated panels accumulate.

Depth readouts use the Jacobian lens (Gurnee et al., Transformer Circuits, 2026), reference implementation anthropics/jacobian-lens (Apache-2.0), hosted by Neuronpedia. The lens is a readout, not an intervention; causal evidence comes from activation patching and steering. The readout is exploratory, reported for one model, and limited to answers inside each layer’s top-8 readout.
