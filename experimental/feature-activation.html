Which features light up — clinical vs patient wording

One scenario, two phrasings. Every SAE feature in the trace, placed by depth and attribution mass, colored by which wording recruits it.

recruited by clinical wording recruited by patient wording shared by both structural scaffolding

Color is read straight off the trace — a feature is green if it carries more attribution mass under clinical wording, ink under patient wording, muted if the two are within a hair. The tall shared peaks are the same topic features in both panels; what flips the answer is the selective set. Clinical wording uniquely lights up allergy, dust, oxygen and lung features and the model answers inhaler. Patient wording instead lights up breathing and clothing features — “tie”, “uniform”, “shirt” — and the answer becomes shirt. Coloring this way sidesteps the auto-tagger entirely: the everyday “attire” feature simply shows ink, because patient wording is what recruits it.
