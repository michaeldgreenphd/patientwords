Model evaluations · four measured models · phrase-deduped statistics

Four models, one measurement.

The same two-phrasing probe, run on every model. The penalty is not one model’s quirk.

Each open-weights model reads the same phrase pairs, one clinical and one in patient wording, and the probability of the clinical next word is measured under both. gemma-2-2b also carries circuit evidence (attribution graphs and features, on the methods page); the other models are measured by direct CPU inference with the identical schema. Every count uses each phrase once per model and is adjusted for multiple comparisons; exact procedures are in the statistical methods note below. One tenth of the newly collected data is sealed off in advance and never touched by these interim numbers; it will check the conclusions once, at the end. The models here are the measured subjects; the stimuli they read are written by Claude models, audited on the methods page.

Statistical methods

One record per (model, clinical phrase); re-traced phrases collapse by majority vote. Penalty intervals: cluster bootstrap, percentile 95%, seed 7, cluster = phrase. Downgrade rates: Clopper–Pearson exact 95%. Asymmetry: exact sign tests, Benjamini–Hochberg corrected across models. Interim numbers use the pre-registered exploration split; a 10% holdout stays sealed until collection ends. Source: data/model_stats.json.

Read this with its limits

Only gemma-2-2b carries circuit evidence; for the other models these are behavioral measurements, next-word probabilities, with no claim about their internals. Care-urgency tier labels follow the reviewed v1 vocabulary (clinician review pending). Measurements are a point in time against fixed open weights. See the broader limitations for what this evidence does and doesn’t show.
