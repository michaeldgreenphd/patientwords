Syntax differences

The words surrounding clinical words can be the same.

A 2×2 factorization crossing the phrasing (patient-derived language vs. medical lexicon, the columns) with the surrounding morphosyntax (standard vs. nonstandard, the rows). The first live-traced item — a simulated insomnia scenario, “insomnia” ↔ “trouble sleeping” crossed with “I've had … I'll finally call” ↔ “I been having … I'ma finally call” — anchors on the prestige box's top prediction “it” (as in call it a night), because the intended target “my doctor” fell below the traced spread. Both shifts push probability toward that everyday reading and away from care-seeking continuations: the variety shift alone adds +11–13% to “it”, the register shift another +4–5%.

Variety shift Δ: +12% toward the everyday continuation · Register shift Δ: +5% (measured on “it”)
